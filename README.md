### Highlights

- **Realistic Traffic Generator**
  - **What it is**: Core service + portal that generates human-like web traffic and signups across multiple client funnels, with strict limits and observability.
  - **Why it matters**: Lets marketing teams warm up and test funnels safely while keeping full control over volume and patterns.

- **AllFresh Farmhouse Ordering**
  - **What it is**: Internal ordering, purchasing, and inventory system for fresh-produce operations (multi-business-unit, virtual warehouses).
  - **Why it matters**: Replaces spreadsheet sprawl with a single source of truth for purchasing, receiving, stock, and shortfall planning.

- **Atlas Logging API**
  - **What it is**: Flask service that unifies leads and SMS conversations, applies AI + rules-based triage, and feeds a work queue for staff.
  - **Why it matters**: Reduces missed urgent cases, standardizes patient communication, and makes staff time more focused.

### How to read these

Each case study is designed to be skimmable in **3–5 minutes**:

- **Problem**: business context, constraints, and what “good” looked like.
- **Solution & architecture**: key design choices, system shape, and trade-offs.
- **Tech stack**: languages, frameworks, data stores, infra, and tooling.
- **Results & impact**: concrete outcomes (performance, reliability, workflow, or revenue/ops impact).

If you’re short on time, start with the top 2–3 items in the index – they’re ordered by **signal and relevance**, not chronology.
